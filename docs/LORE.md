# EMBERVALE

## Master Lore & Core Game Design Document

---

# Overview

**Title:** Embervale

**Genre:** Open World Fantasy Action RPG

**Perspective:** First Person

**Setting:** High Fantasy World in Decline

**Tone:** Heroic Fantasy with Mythological and Dark Fantasy Elements

**Core Themes:**

* Hope in the face of extinction
* The burden of destiny
* Power versus corruption
* Sacrifice
* Legacy
* Redemption
* The cycle of creation and destruction

---

# Game Premise

For a thousand years the world has been dying.

The gods are dead.

Magic is fading.

Ancient kingdoms crumble.

Dragons vanish from the skies.

Monsters roam lands once protected by divine power.

Long ago, seven chosen champions known as the Flamebearers were created to restore balance and defeat the god responsible for the world's downfall.

Six failed.

Some fell to corruption.

Some sought power.

Some became tyrants.

Some willingly joined the enemy.

Now only one remains.

You are the Seventh Flamebearer.

The final hope of Embervale.

Or its final doom.

---

# The World of Embervale

The world was once united under the protection of seven gods.

Following a divine war known as The Shattering, civilization fractured into four major realms.

Each realm preserves a different piece of the world's lost glory.

Each is threatened by a fallen Flamebearer.

---

# The Four Realms

## The Ember Crown

The heartland of humanity.

Rolling plains.

Ancient castles.

Knightly orders.

Large cities.

This realm still represents hope and civilization.

Major Threat:
The Iron King

A former Flamebearer who believes freedom must be sacrificed for survival.

---

## Frostfang Reach

Northern wilderness.

Frozen oceans.

Towering mountain ranges.

Ancient dragon nests.

Home of warrior clans and beast races.

Major Threat:
The Storm Tyrant

A Flamebearer consumed by vengeance against the gods.

---

## The Ashen Wilds

A scarred wasteland created during the Divine Cataclysm.

Ruins of forgotten civilizations.

Monster-infested territories.

Corrupted forests.

Dangerous magical anomalies.

Major Threat:
The Beast Lord

A former hero who embraced corruption and seeks humanity's extinction.

---

## The Sunspire Dominion

Ancient deserts.

Lost jungles.

Buried temples.

Dragon cults.

The oldest surviving civilization.

Keeper of the last great libraries.

Major Threat:
The Crimson Prophet

A Flamebearer who created a religion dedicated to the Ash King.

# The Divine Cataclysm

Thousands of years ago Morthul declared that existence itself was flawed.

Everything dies.

Everything ends.

Even gods.

He believed creation should be destroyed and reborn.

The other gods resisted.

The resulting war shattered the world.

Continents broke apart.

Oceans changed course.

Entire civilizations vanished.

Six gods died.

Morthul survived.

The world has been dying ever since.

---

# The Flamebearers

Before their deaths the gods created seven champions.

These individuals became known as the Flamebearers.

Their mission:

Restore balance.

Defeat Morthul.

Save creation.

Each carried a fragment of divine power.

Each possessed the potential to become humanity's greatest hero.

Six failed.

# Core Gameplay

## Combat

Primary Inspiration:

* Skyrim
* Elden Ring

Combat Philosophy:

Easy to learn.

Difficult to master.

Heavy weapon impact.

Precise timing.

Meaningful enemy encounters.

No button mashing.

---

## Progression

Players improve through:

* Combat experience
* Quest completion
* Divine relic acquisition
* Equipment upgrades
* Companion development

No traditional class lock.

Players create their own build.

---

Players may purchase and claim:

* Homes

# The Corruption System

The defining mechanic of Embervale.

Every defeated Flamebearer grants the player new power.

However:

Power comes with corruption.

As corruption rises:

Appearance changes.

Dialogue options evolve.

NPC reactions shift.

Abilities become darker.

The player increasingly resembles previous fallen heroes.

The central question becomes:

Can the Seventh Flamebearer resist the fate that consumed the others?

# Main Story Structure

## Act I – Awakening

The player discovers their identity as the Seventh Flamebearer.

Ancient forces begin hunting them.

The journey begins.

---

## Act II – Gathering the Flame

Travel across all four realms.

Acquire divine relics.

Build alliances.

Defeat fallen Flamebearers.

---

## Act III – Truth of the Gods

The player learns the history of the Divine Cataclysm.

Discovers the true nature of Morthul.

Learns that someone must always sit upon the Ash Throne.

---

## Act IV – The Celestial War

Assault the ruined Celestial Realm.

Defeat the Ashen Knight.

Confront Morthul.

Choose the world's future.

---

# Ending One: Dawnfire

The player rejects ultimate power.

Uses the relics to restore balance.

The lands heal.

Dragons return.

Magic slowly recovers.

Civilization enters a new golden age.

The Seventh Flamebearer becomes legend.

The Age of Ash ends.

The Age of Dawn begins.

---

# Ending Two: Lord of Embers

The player embraces corruption.

Kills Morthul.

Claims the Ash Throne.

Becomes the new god of the world.

Order is restored through absolute power.

The world survives.

But freedom dies.

The Age of Ash ends.

The Age of Embers begins.

---

# Core Vision Statement

Embervale is a first-person open-world fantasy RPG where players explore a beautiful but dying world, battle fallen heroes, forge powerful alliances, uncover the secrets of dead gods, and decide whether they will become the savior of creation or the next Ash King.
